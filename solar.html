What is solar energy?


Solar energy is power derived from the sun's radiation. It's harnessed through various technologies like solar panels, which convert sunlight into electricity or heat for homes, businesses, and even satellites.


How it works


There are two types of technology that convert the sun's light into usable electricity.


  • Photovoltaics - These make up more of the solar panels most people are familiar with, they are composed of small sheets called cells which are made from a semiconducting material. When photons from sunlight strike the cells, they excite electrons in the semiconductor material, creating an electric current. These cells are often less than the thickness of four human hairs, so in order to withstand the outdoors for many years, cells are sandwiched between protective materials in a combination of glass and/or plastics.

  • Concentrating Solar Power (CSP) - Concentrating Solar power systems are mostly used for large scale solar plants. This variation of solar power uses mirrors to direct sunlight to a small area generating lots of heat. As the sun's rays hit these mirrors, they reflect and concentrate sunlight onto a central receiver, typically located at the top of a tower. The heat from the concentrated sunlight heats a fluid to very high temperatures, this fluid is then used to generate steam. This steam then drives a turbine which generates electricity.


Costs to consider


The costs associated with installing solar panels can vary significantly based on a multitude of factors, shaping both the initial investment and long-term savings for homeowners and businesses alike.
